Auditory Communication Enhancement Through Touch

ACETT (Auditory Communication Enhancement Through Touch) enables listening through touch by converting the sounds of the environment including speech, into vibrations that the users feel on their hands. Deaf and Hard of Hearing People receive a unique sound experience through the device, which enhances their awareness of the sounds around them

What ACETT does

ACETT turns sounds into vibrations—allowing Deaf and Hard‑of‑Hearing individuals to experience auditory information through touch.

  • Converts speech and environmental sounds to tactile patterns in real time.
  • Designed for inclusive learning, situational awareness, and natural communication.
  • Built from the HAIDWARE × Irerobot collaboration for reliability and innovation.
Advanced sound sensing
Array microphones capture speech and ambient cues.
Signal processing
Proprietary DSP extracts features and encodes patterns.

Haptics deliver clear, comfortable vibrations on skin.

Technology

Using advanced sound sensors, proprietary signal processing, and a tactile feedback interface, ACETT translates speech and environmental sounds into real‑time vibrations on the user’s skin.

  • Sensors → capture acoustic input
  • DSP → extract features & map to haptic language
  • Haptics → render patterns as distinct, learnable vibrations
Built to high safety & performance standards; engineered with certification in mind.
